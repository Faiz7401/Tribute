Tunku Abdul Rahman

Bapa Kemerdekaan

Here's a time line of Tunku Abdul Rahman's life:

  • 1903 - Tunku Abdul Rahman was born on February 8 in Alor Setar, Kedah, Malaysia, into the royal family of Kedah. His full name is Tunku Abdul Rahman Putra Al-Haj.
  • 1925 - He went to the United Kingdom to study at the University of Cambridge. He was one of the first Malaysians to receive a Western education.
  • 1929 - Graduated from Cambridge with a degree in law. He later joined the British Colonial Service, serving in various administrative roles in Malaya.
  • 1931 - He was appointed the Assistant District Officer in Kedah, marking the beginning of his career in public service.
  • 1945 - Following World War II, Tunku became involved in the political scene. He was appointed to the first official Malay delegation to London to discuss post-war independence for Malaya.
  • 1946 - Tunku was appointed the first president of the United Malays National Organisation (UMNO), a political party that would play a significant role in Malaysia's independence.
  • 1951 - Tunku became the Chief Minister of the Federated Malay States, pushing forward his vision of independence from British colonial rule.
  • 1955 - As the Chief Minister, Tunku led the negotiations with the British government, leading to the establishment of the Federation of Malaya. He became the first Malayan Prime Minister after the country's independence.
  • 1957 - On August 31, Tunku Abdul Rahman led the Federation of Malaya to independence from British rule, becoming the first Prime Minister of Malaysia. His famous declaration, "Merdeka" (Freedom), marked a new era for the country.
  • 1963 - Tunku helped to establish the Federation of Malaysia, which included Malaya, Singapore, and the East Malaysian states of Sabah and Sarawak. Singapore left the federation in 1965.
  • 1965 - Despite the separation of Singapore from Malaysia, Tunku maintained strong diplomatic relations and worked to strengthen Malaysia’s international standing.
  • 1970 - After serving as Prime Minister for 13 years, Tunku Abdul Rahman retired from politics, passing the leadership to Tun Abdul Razak.
  • 1990 - Tunku Abdul Rahman passed away on December 6 at the age of 87. He was remembered for his leadership in securing Malaysia’s independence and his vision for a united and peaceful country.
